Carbon Sequestration

The carbon sequestration potential index (CSPI) is an index to express carbon uptake efficiency. The index is calculated using the gross primary production (GPP), which is the rate at which carbon in the atmosphere is fixed by photosynthesis. Aboveground carbon density (ACD) is also used, which is the carbon stored in living plant tissues on the earth surface. Combining these concepts can help determine which areas should be protected and which need reforestation in order to absorb more atmospheric carbon. It can also help with SDG 15, life on land.

Based on the GPP map above, the darker red spaces should be conserved, whereas the lighter ones could benefit from increased vegetation in terms of carbon absorption.

Making the map

To start, a shapefile of the region of interest and GeoTIFF file from the ESA biomass dataset for 2020 were imported to google earth engine (GEE). A preprogrammed GEE code was used to process the data. I then used a GEE tutorial by Chackraborty to format the web app.
