3D map of Utrecht

For the fun of it, I thought I would also try modeling Utrecht as a 3D map. This can be useful for spotting locations with high structures, which is important for planes. In this map, I used the brighter red and yellow colors to highlight low elevations. This can show areas that might be vulnerable to flooding during extreme rainstorm conditions. Since climate change could amplify the hydrologic cycle this seems like a relevant map for the Netherlands. An added benefit for the 3D map is that itâ€™s visually appealing and interactive, such that the information could come across to the public better, and with more effect.

In short, I did this using the DSM and point cloud data. I added these as layers in QGIS, and then created a 3D map view to show the city in 3D. I then learned how to make an animation by collecting images in the 3D map view, and compiling them in GIMP. Though I was not able to zoom in or move around very well, likely due to insufficient RAM or CPU on my laptop, the results were pretty cool!
