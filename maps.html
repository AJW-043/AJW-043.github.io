What is GIS?

Geographical information science combines computational science with the social and natural sciences to study geographic information. This discipline frequently makes use of geographic information systems (GIS). These manage, analyze and visualize geographic data, which are very practical for understanding the world around us and how it changes. For instance, through GIS we can understand which areas are most influential in absorbing atmospheric carbon and should thus be preserved in our efforts to combat climate change. GIS can also help in tracking our progress in sustainable development and identifying areas that need more focus. The applications are vast, a few of which are touched upon in my maps below. About the course

The maps were made following lab assignments during a two week long GIS summer course. The course was taught by Dr. Britta Ricker at the University College Utrecht. The aim was to use a variety of mapping tools to visualize different issues in sustainability. From importing data to making web maps, I gained valuable experience throughout the course.
